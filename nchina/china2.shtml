Xinjiang

Xinjiang Province covers a vast area of central asia bordered by Mongolia on the east, Siberia to the north, Tibet to the south and the landlocked "Stans" in the west. My mission there was to visit coal mines and sample the coal for possible exploitation by new clean technoligies being developed at our Houston research center. In the course of the work I had the pleasure to drive across the region from Hami in the east to Urumqi and Manas in the west and see many sights in-between.

Below are four views along that stretch of country.

Buddhist temple perched above the waters of the fabeled Heavanly Lake in the high Bogda Shan range east of Urumqi.

Visiting the ancient central asian city of Gaochang in the Turpan basin.

Ancient hand-dug underground irregation tunnels bringing fresh water from the mountains to the desert city of Turpan.

The team at work! Kevin, Miss Li, and Jerry collecting coal samples from the open pit mine at Sandoling west of Hami.
